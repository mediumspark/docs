The **Saved Drafts** panel gives you a central place to see all your drafts — both those stored in your browser and those synced to your account. Access it from the navigation bar at the top of the page.

## Opening the Saved Drafts panel

Click **⭐ Saved Drafts** in the top navigation bar. The panel opens and displays two tabs:

- **💻 Local Drafts** — drafts stored in your current browser's local storage
- **☁ My Account Drafts** — drafts synced to your QuickDraft account (requires login)

## Local Drafts

The **Local Drafts** tab lists every draft saved to your current browser's local storage.

- Click any draft in the list to open it in the editor.
- Click **Clear All Local** to delete all locally stored drafts.

<Warning>
  **Clear All Local** permanently deletes every draft in your browser's local storage. This action cannot be undone. Export or sync important drafts to your account before clearing.
</Warning>

## Account Drafts

The **☁ My Account Drafts** tab lists every draft you've saved to your QuickDraft account.

- You must be logged in to view this tab.
- Account drafts are accessible from any browser or device where you're signed in — they are not tied to a single machine.
- See the [Cloud Sync guide](/guides/account-sync) to learn how to save a draft to your account.

## Favoriting a draft

Mark a draft as a favorite to pin it to the top of the Saved Drafts list for quick access.

1. Open the draft you want to favorite.
2. Click the **♡** (heart) icon in the toolbar. The icon fills to indicate the draft is favorited.
3. Open **Saved Drafts** — the favorited draft now appears at the top of the list.
4. Click the **♡** icon again to remove the favorite and return the draft to its normal position.

<Tip>
  Give important drafts a custom URL slug (for example, `project-brief` or `meeting-notes-jan`) before saving them. Descriptive slugs make drafts easy to identify at a glance in the Saved Drafts list.
</Tip>
