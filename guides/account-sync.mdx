---

QuickDraft's free account lets you save drafts to the cloud and access them from any device. Unlike local storage, cloud-synced drafts persist across browsers and survive browser data clearing — your writing is always there when you need it.

## Create a free account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Sign Up page">
    Click **Sign Up** in the top navigation bar on [quickdraft.in](https://www.quickdraft.in).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your details">
    Enter your name, email address, and a password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the form">
    Click **Sign Up** to create your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start saving">
    You're now logged in and ready to save drafts to your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Save a draft to your account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the draft">
    Open the draft you want to save in the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trigger the save dialog">
    Click the **☁** (cloud) icon in the toolbar, or click **☁ Save to My Account** below the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a title">
    Enter a title for the draft in the dialog that appears. Choose something descriptive so you can identify it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the save">
    Click **Save Draft**. The draft is now stored in your account and accessible from any device.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Access your drafts from another device

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open QuickDraft">
    Open [https://www.quickdraft.in](https://www.quickdraft.in) on another device or browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log in">
    Click **Log In** in the top navigation bar and enter your email and password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open your Account Drafts">
    Click **⭐ Saved Drafts** in the navigation bar, then switch to the **☁ My Account Drafts** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a draft">
    Click any draft in the list to open it in the editor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Signing in and out

- Click **Log In** in the top navigation bar to sign in with your email and password.
- Click **Log Out** to sign out of your account. All your account drafts remain safe in the cloud and will be there the next time you log in.

<Note>
  Local drafts and account drafts are separate. Local drafts exist only in the browser where they were created; account drafts are stored in the cloud. You can save any local draft to your account at any time by clicking the **☁** cloud icon while viewing it — keeping both a local copy and a cloud backup.
</Note>
