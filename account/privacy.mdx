QuickDraft is built with privacy as a default. Whether you write anonymously or with an account, your draft content is never used for advertising, never shared with third parties, and never read by QuickDraft's team.

## Where your data is stored

- **Without an account:** Your drafts are saved to your browser's local storage only. They never leave your device unless you deliberately share a link.
- **With an account:** Drafts you explicitly save to your account are stored on QuickDraft's servers, encrypted at rest. Only you can access them when signed in.

## What QuickDraft does NOT collect

- No ad tracking cookies
- No personal data beyond your account email and name (only if you sign up)
- No reading or analysis of your draft content
- No selling of data to third parties

## Sharing and public access

When you share an edit link or a read-only link, anyone with that URL can access your draft. Keep this in mind when sharing sensitive content.

- **Read-only link:** Recipients can view the draft but cannot make edits.
- **Password protection:** Adds a layer of access control — only people with the correct password can view or edit the draft. See the Password Protection guide for setup instructions.

<Note>
  QuickDraft staff cannot view your password-protected drafts. The password is enforced at the application level and draft content is not accessible to the team.
</Note>

## Deleting your data

- **Local drafts:** Open the **Saved Drafts** panel and click **Clear All Local** to permanently delete all drafts stored in your browser.
- **Account drafts:** Open the **My Account Drafts** panel and delete individual drafts as needed.
- **Full account deletion:** To delete your account and all associated data entirely, contact support at the address listed in the site footer.

<Note>
  For the full legal privacy policy, visit [https://www.quickdraft.in/privacy.php](https://www.quickdraft.in/privacy.php).
</Note>
