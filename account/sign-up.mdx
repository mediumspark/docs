A QuickDraft account is completely optional — you can write and share drafts without ever signing up. But creating a free account unlocks cloud sync: your drafts are saved to the cloud and available from any browser or device whenever you're signed in.

## Create your account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to QuickDraft">
    Navigate to [https://www.quickdraft.in](https://www.quickdraft.in) and click **Sign Up** in the top navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your details">
    Enter your name, email address, and a password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm sign-up">
    Click **Sign Up** to create your account and log in automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you get with an account

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Cloud sync" icon="cloud">
    Save drafts to the cloud and access them from any device, any time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cross-device access" icon="laptop-mobile">
    Open your drafts on your phone, tablet, or desktop — wherever you are.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Draft library" icon="folder-open">
    Browse all your saved drafts in one place from the My Account Drafts panel.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Log in to your account

1. Click **Log In** in the top navigation bar.
2. Enter your email and password, then click **Log In**.
3. Your cloud drafts are available under **Saved Drafts → ☁ My Account Drafts**.

## Anonymous drafts vs. account drafts

| | Anonymous drafts | Account drafts |
|---|---|---|
| **Storage** | Browser local storage | QuickDraft cloud |
| **Device access** | That browser only | Any device when signed in |
| **Survives browser wipe** | No | Yes |

- **Anonymous drafts** are stored in your browser's local storage and tied to that specific browser only.
- **Account drafts** are saved to the cloud and accessible from any device when you're signed in.
- You can upgrade any anonymous draft to an account draft at any time by clicking **☁ Save to My Account** inside the editor.

<Note>
  QuickDraft does not send marketing emails. Your email address is used only for account access and password recovery — nothing else.
</Note>
