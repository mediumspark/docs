QuickDraft saves your work automatically as you type. Every keystroke is written to your browser's local storage, so your draft is still there if you close the tab, navigate away, or your browser crashes — no manual saving required.

## How auto-save works

- Each draft is stored under its unique URL slug in your browser's local storage. Two different slugs mean two independent drafts.
- The last-modified timestamp at the bottom of the editor updates with every save, so you always know when your content was last written.
- No internet connection is needed for auto-save — the entire process runs locally in your browser.

## Force a manual save

Click **Save Data** below the editor to immediately flush the current draft to local storage.

This is useful when you want to:
- Confirm a save before sharing a link with someone
- Snapshot the current content before switching devices
- Ensure the latest changes are stored before closing the browser

## Limitations of local storage

Local storage is convenient, but it has a few important constraints to keep in mind:

- **Browser-specific:** Drafts saved in Chrome are not available in Firefox on the same computer. Each browser maintains its own isolated storage.
- **Cache clearing:** Clearing your browser's cache or site data will permanently delete all locally stored drafts.
- **Storage capacity:** Local storage capacity varies by browser but is typically 5–10 MB. Very large drafts may approach this limit.
- **Single device:** Local drafts don't follow you across devices. Use a free account and **Save to My Account** for permanent, cross-device storage.

<Warning>
  QuickDraft cannot recover drafts lost due to browser data clearing. To prevent data loss, save important drafts to your account using the cloud sync feature, or export them as `.txt` files before clearing your browser.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Bookmark the draft's URL in your browser. The URL is tied to the slug under which the content is saved in local storage, so returning to that URL always loads your saved draft.
</Tip>
