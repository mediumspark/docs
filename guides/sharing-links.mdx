---

Every draft in QuickDraft has a unique URL. You can share it as an editable link so collaborators can contribute, or as a read-only link so viewers can read without making changes.

## Share an edit link

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your draft">
    Open your draft in the editor at [quickdraft.in](https://www.quickdraft.in).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the edit link">
    Below the editor, click **Copy Edit Link**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the link">
    Paste the link and send it to anyone you want to collaborate with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collaborators start editing">
    Recipients open the link and can immediately start editing the draft alongside you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Anyone with the edit link can modify the draft. Use [password protection](/guides/password-protection) for sensitive content.
</Warning>

## Share a read-only link

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy the read-only link">
    Below the editor, click **Copy Read-Only Link**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the link">
    Paste and share the link with your intended audience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recipients read, not edit">
    Recipients can read the draft but cannot make any edits.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Read-only links are great for:

- Distributing meeting notes to a wider team
- Sharing step-by-step instructions with an audience
- Publishing draft announcements for review before they go live

## Share the canonical URL

Click **Copy Share URL** to copy the direct link to your draft. This is the same URL shown in your browser's address bar. Share it on social platforms, in emails, or via any messaging app to point people directly to your draft.

## Share on social platforms

Below the editor, dedicated one-click buttons let you share the draft URL directly on:

- **Facebook**
- **WhatsApp**
- **Reddit**
- **Telegram**
- **Twitter/X**
- **Email**

Click any button and the platform opens with your draft URL pre-filled, ready to post or send.

<Tip>
  Set a [custom URL slug](/guides/custom-urls) before sharing to make your link easy to remember and type — for example, `quickdraft.in/team-standup` is far more shareable than a random ID.
</Tip>
