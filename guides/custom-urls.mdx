By default, QuickDraft assigns a random ID to every draft. You can replace it with a custom slug — a short, meaningful keyword that makes the URL easy to share and remember, like `quickdraft.in/team-standup` or `quickdraft.in/launch-checklist`.

## Set a custom URL

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your draft">
    Open your draft in the editor at [quickdraft.in](https://www.quickdraft.in).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Custom URL">
    Below the editor, click **🔗 Custom URL**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your slug">
    In the dialog, enter your desired slug. Slugs must be lowercase and may only contain letters, numbers, and hyphens — for example, `my-meeting-notes`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the slug">
    Click **Apply**. The draft URL updates immediately in your browser's address bar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Slug rules

Custom slugs must follow these constraints:

| Rule | Allowed |
|---|---|
| Letters | Lowercase only (a–z) |
| Numbers | 0–9 |
| Separators | Hyphens (`-`) |
| Spaces | ✗ Not allowed |
| Underscores | ✗ Not allowed |
| Capital letters | ✗ Not allowed |
| Special characters | ✗ Not allowed |

**Example:**

```text
my-project-notes   ✓
My_Project Notes!  ✗
```

## Sharing custom URLs

Once you've set a custom slug, share the URL just like any other draft link:

- Copy it directly from your browser's address bar, or
- Click **Copy Share URL** below the editor

Custom URLs are easier to type from memory and work particularly well in presentations, emails, or printed materials where long random strings are impractical.

## Changing or removing a slug

To update your slug at any time:

1. Click **🔗 Custom URL** below the editor again.
2. Enter a new slug and click **Apply**.

<Warning>
  Old URLs pointing to the previous slug will no longer work after you change it. Update any links you've already shared to avoid broken references.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  Custom slugs are global across all QuickDraft drafts. If your chosen slug is already taken by another user, you'll need to choose a different one.
</Warning>
