QuickDraft includes voice-to-text dictation powered by your browser's built-in speech recognition. Click the microphone icon to start speaking — your words are transcribed directly into the editor in real time, with no plugins or external accounts required.

## Starting dictation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the microphone">
    Click the microphone icon (🎤) in the editor toolbar to activate voice input.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant microphone permission">
    Your browser may display a permission prompt the first time you use this feature. Click **Allow** to let QuickDraft access your microphone. If you accidentally clicked **Block**, update the permission in your browser's site settings and reload the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak your draft">
    Speak clearly toward your microphone. Your words appear in the editor as you speak — you can watch the transcription build in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop dictation">
    Click the **✕** (stop) button that appears next to the microphone icon to end the dictation session. The transcribed text remains in the editor, ready for you to review and edit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Browser compatibility

Voice-to-text in QuickDraft uses the **Web Speech API**, which is built into modern browsers. No third-party service or internet connection is required for the transcription itself.

The following browsers support voice dictation:

- **Google Chrome** — desktop and Android
- **Microsoft Edge** — desktop
- **Safari** — macOS and iOS (if the microphone prompt does not appear, check that microphone access for Safari is enabled under your device's **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone**)

<Warning>
  **Firefox does not support the Web Speech API.** Voice dictation will not be available if you open QuickDraft in Firefox. Switch to Chrome or Edge for full voice-to-text support.
</Warning>

## Tips for better accuracy

Get the most out of voice dictation by following these practices:

- **Choose a quiet environment.** Background noise is the most common cause of transcription errors.
- **Speak at a natural, steady pace.** Speaking too quickly reduces accuracy; there's no need to rush.
- **Use punctuation words** where your browser supports them — say "period", "comma", "question mark", or "new paragraph" to insert punctuation and line breaks without touching the keyboard.
- **Review after dictating.** Speech recognition is not perfect — always read through your transcription and correct any misheard words before sharing or publishing.

<Note>
  QuickDraft never sends your audio to its own servers. All speech recognition is handled entirely inside your browser using the Web Speech API, so your voice data stays on your device.
</Note>
